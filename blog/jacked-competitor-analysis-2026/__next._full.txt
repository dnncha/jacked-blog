<h2 id="executive-summary">Executive Summary</h2>
<p>The fitness app market is valued at $15B+ globally. Jacked competes in the <strong>strength/hypertrophy training</strong> niche — a segment dominated by generalist workout trackers. Key differentiator: <strong>autoprogression + fatigue management</strong> for serious lifters.</p>
<p>| Competitor | Revenue | Key Differentiator | Target |
|------------|---------|-------------------|--------|
| <strong>Sweat</strong> | $100M/yr | Influencer brand, women | Casual fitness |
| <strong>Strong</strong> | ~$7M/yr | Workout logging, clean UI | Intermediate lifters |
| <strong>Hevy</strong> | $2M/yr (~$160K MRR) | Social features, community | Intermediate lifters |
| <strong>GymStreak</strong> | $2.5M/yr | AI-generated programs | Intermediate-advanced |
| <strong>Fitbod</strong> | ~$1M | AI personalization | All levels |
| <strong>JEFIT</strong> | ~$1M | Social features, community | Intermediate |
| <strong>StrongLifts</strong> | Unknown (bootstrapped) | Simplicity, 5x5 focus | Beginners |
| <strong>Liftosaur</strong> | Unknown (small) | Programmability, advanced | Advanced lifters |</p>

<h3 id="1-sweat-by-kayla-itsines">1. Sweat (by Kayla Itsines)</h3>
<p><strong>What they are:</strong>
The biggest fitness app in the world. Female-focused, influencer-backed, comprehensive training + nutrition + mindset.</p>
<p>| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| <strong>Founded</strong> | 2015 |
| <strong>Founder</strong> | Kayla Itsines (influencer) + Tom Billington (tech) |
| <strong>Headquarters</strong> | Australia |
| <strong>Revenue</strong> | ~$100M/year |
| <strong>Users</strong> | 50M+ members |
| <strong>Sale price</strong> | $400M (sold 2023) |
| <strong>Funding</strong> | Bootstrapped + VC (later) |
| <strong>Marketing</strong> | Influencer marketing, Instagram-first, brand partnerships |
| <strong>Pricing</strong> | ~$19.99/mo |
| <strong>Key strength</strong> | Brand, community, influencer reach |
| <strong>Weakness</strong> | Not strength-focused, expensive |</p>
<p><strong>Takeaway:</strong> Not direct competition (different niche), but shows what's possible with brand + community.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="2-strong-strongapp-com">2. Strong (strongapp.com)</h3>
<p><strong>What they are:</strong>
Clean, minimalist workout tracker. One of the top-grossing fitness apps globally.</p>
<p>| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| <strong>Founded</strong> | 2017 |
| <strong>Founders</strong> | (Acquired by EXPA) |

| <strong>Downloads</strong> | 10M+ |
| <strong>Funding</strong> | Raised (amount unknown) |
| <strong>Marketing</strong> | App Store optimization, product-led growth |
| <strong>Pricing</strong> | $12.99/mo or $79.99/yr |
| <strong>Key strength</strong> | Clean UI, brand recognition |
| <strong>Weakness</strong> | Generic — no autoprogression, no AI |</p>
<p><strong>Takeaway:</strong> The "default" choice for serious lifters. Good UX, but Jacked's autoprogression is a genuine differentiator.</p>

<hr>
<h2 id="jacked-s-position">Jacked's Position</h2>
<p><strong>Where Jacked wins:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Autoprogression (automatic weight increases) — not offered by Strong or Hevy</li>
<li>Fatigue management — unique in the market</li>
<li>Hypertrophy focus — not a generic fitness app</li>
<li>Clean positioning: "serious lifters only"</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Threats:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>GymStreak is already doing AI + hypertrophy</li>
<li>Strong has brand recognition</li>
<li>Hevy has community locked in</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Opportunity:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Own the "autoprogression" category</li>
<li>Build content flywheel (the blog is already doing this)</li>
<li>Target the Reddit r/weightroom r/fitness crowd</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="key-takeaways-for-jacked">Key Takeaways for Jacked</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>$2-3M is achievable</strong> — GymStreak and Hevy prove bootstrapped fitness apps can hit this</li>
<li><strong>Community matters</strong> — Hevy grew to $2M with Reddit + social, no paid ads</li>
<li><strong>AI is competitive</strong> — GymStreak and Fitbod own this space</li>
<li><strong>Simplicity wins</strong> — StrongLifts proves one good program beats feature bloat</li>
<li><strong>Autoprogression is defensible</strong> — No major competitor offers this</li>
</ol>
<hr>
<h2 id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Double down on autoprogression</strong> — It's your unique position</li>
<li><strong>Go where lifters hang out</strong> — Reddit r/weightroom, fitness Discord</li>
<li><strong>Content is working</strong> — The blog + YouTube pipeline is correct</li>
<li><strong>Consider GymStreak as closest rival</strong> — They're winning with AI + hypertrophy</li>
<li><strong>Target $1M ARR</strong> — Realistic 2-3 year goal</li>
</ol>
