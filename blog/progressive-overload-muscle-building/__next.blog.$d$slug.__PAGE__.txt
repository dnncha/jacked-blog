e:Td26,<p>If there's one principle that determines whether you build muscle, it's progressive overload. Everything else — exercise selection, rep ranges, rest periods — matters less than this.</p>
<p>But here's the problem: most people understand progressive overload incorrectly. They think it just means "lift more weight." That's part of it, but it's not the whole picture.</p>
<h2 id="what-progressive-overload-actually-means">What Progressive Overload Actually Means</h2>
<p>Progressive overload means systematically increasing the demands placed on your muscles over time. Your body adapts to stress. To keep growing, you must increase that stress.</p>
<p>This can happen through:</p>
<p><strong>1. Adding Weight</strong>
The most obvious form. Last week you benched 100kg for 8 reps. This week you do 102.5kg for 8.</p>
<p><strong>2. Adding Reps</strong>
Can't add weight? Do more reps with the same weight. If 100kg × 8 is hard, aim for 100kg × 9 next week.</p>
<p><strong>3. Adding Sets</strong>
More total volume = more growth stimulus. Adding a set per exercise each week compounds over time.</p>
<p><strong>4. Improving Form</strong>
Better technique = more tension on the target muscle. This is progressive overload too.</p>
<p><strong>5. Decreasing Rest</strong>
Same weights and reps, but with shorter rest periods = harder workout.</p>
<h2 id="the-progressive-overload-problem">The Progressive Overload Problem</h2>
<p>Here's where most apps fail: they leave you to figure out your progression yourself. You're supposed to remember what you lifted last time, calculate the increase, and track it all.</p>
<p>That's cognitive overhead that gets in the way of training.</p>
<h2 id="how-jacked-handles-progressive-overload">How Jacked Handles Progressive Overload</h2>
<p>Jacked automates progression so you don't have to think about it:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Auto-weight suggestions</strong>: The app knows what you lifted last session and suggests a weight increase when you hit your rep targets</li>
<li><strong>Rep goal tracking</strong>: Hit your target reps? The app knows. It adjusts automatically.</li>
<li><strong>Weekly volume increases</strong>: Jacked tracks your sets per muscle group and progresses you when you're ready</li>
<li><strong>Deload management</strong>: When you're approaching overreaching, the app suggests backing off — so you don't stall</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="how-to-apply-it-yourself">How to Apply It Yourself</h2>
<p>If you're not using Jacked, here's how to progress:</p>
<p><strong>Weekly Progression Template:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Week 1: 3 sets × 8 reps at RPE 7</li>
<li>Week 2: 3 sets × 8 reps at RPE 8</li>
<li>Week 3: 3 sets × 8 reps at RPE 9</li>
<li>Week 4: Deload (reduce weight by 20%, same reps)</li>
<li>Week 5: Start 5% heavier than Week 1</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Track Everything:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Weight, reps, sets for every exercise</li>
<li>Weekly volume per muscle group</li>
<li>RPE or RIR for each set</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="the-bottom-line">The Bottom Line</h2>
<p>Progressive overload isn't optional. It's the only game in town for muscle growth.</p>
<p>The question is: are you tracking it properly, or just hoping you'll remember what to lift?</p>
