e:T9a7,<p>Six months ago you added 10lbs to your bench. Now you're stuck at the same weight for weeks.</p>
<p>Welcome to the plateau. Here's why it happens — and how to break through.</p>
<h2 id="why-you-plateau">Why You Plateau</h2>
<p><strong>1. Your Body Adapted</strong>
The same stimulus produces the same adaptation. Do the same workout with the same weights = same results.</p>
<p><strong>2. No Progressive Overload</strong>
Muscle grows when challenged beyond current capacity. If weights stay the same, growth stops.</p>
<p><strong>3. Accumulated Fatigue</strong>
You've been pushing hard for months. Your recovery is compromised. You're training but not growing.</p>
<p><strong>4. Volume Stall</strong>
You've hit your muscle's volume ceiling. More of the same = no extra growth.</p>
<h2 id="how-to-break-through">How to Break Through</h2>
<h3 id="option-1-increase-progressive-overload">Option 1: Increase Progressive Overload</h3>
<ul>
<li>Add weight (if you can)</li>
<li>Add reps with same weight</li>
<li>Add sets</li>
<li>Decrease rest time</li>
<li>Improve form/tempo</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="option-2-change-volume-distribution">Option 2: Change Volume Distribution</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hit under-trained muscles harder</li>
<li>Increase frequency</li>
<li>Drop volume on over-trained muscles</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="option-3-deload">Option 3: Deload</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduce weight by 20% for 1-2 weeks</li>
<li>Let recovery catch up</li>
<li>Come back stronger</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="option-4-use-an-app-that-adapts">Option 4: Use an App That Adapts</h3>
<p>Let's be honest: manual tracking is tedious. An app that automatically progresses you, tracks volume per muscle, and manages fatigue makes plateaus rare.</p>
<h2 id="the-real-problem">The Real Problem</h2>
<p>Most people plateau because:</p>
<ul>
<li>They don't track progression consistently</li>
<li>They don't track volume per muscle</li>
<li>They don't manage fatigue</li>
</ul>
<p>Fix these three things and plateaus become rare.</p>

</ul>
