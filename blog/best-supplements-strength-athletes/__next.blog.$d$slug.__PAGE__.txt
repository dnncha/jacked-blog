e:T106e,<p>Supplement marketing is full of promises. Here's what's actually backed by science.</p>
<h2 id="tier-1-definitely-works">Tier 1: Definitely Works</h2>
<h3 id="creatine-monohydrate">Creatine Monohydrate</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★★★★ | 500+ studies
<strong>Dose:</strong> 3-5g daily
<strong>Cost:</strong> €15-20/month</p>
<p>The king of supplements. Increases phosphocreatine stores, allowing more reps at high intensity. Benefits strength, power, and recovery. Cheap, safe, effective.</p>
<p><a href="/blog/creatine-loading-phase-necessary">Learn more about creatine loading</a></p>
<h3 id="caffeine">Caffeine</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★★★★ | 400+ studies<br>
<strong>Dose:</strong> 100-200mg pre-workout
**Cost5-10/month</p>
<p>Power:** €ful ergogenic aid. Improves power output, endurance, and workout volume. Tolerance builds quickly — cycle off periodically.</p>
<h3 id="whey-protein">Whey Protein</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★★★☆ | Extensive
<strong>Dose:</strong> 20-40g as needed
<strong>Cost:</strong> €20-30/month</p>
<p>Not a magic supplement — just convenient protein. Helps hit daily protein targets. Useful for post-workout or busy days.</p>
<h2 id="tier-2-probably-works">Tier 2: Probably Works</h2>
<h3 id="beta-alanine">Beta-Alanine</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★★☆☆ | 50+ studies
<strong>Dose:</strong> 3-5g daily
<strong>Cost:</strong> €10-15/month</p>
<p>Buffers lactic acid. Improves muscular endurance. The tingly sensation (paresthesia) is harmless but annoying.</p>
<h3 id="citrulline-malate">Citrulline Malate</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★★☆☆ | 30+ studies
<strong>Dose:</strong> 6-8g
<strong>Cost:</strong> €15-20/month</p>
<p>Improves blood flow and endurance. May help with pump and workout volume.</p>
<p><a href="/blog/citrulline-supplementation-strength-training">More on citrulline</a></p>
<h3 id="ashwagandha">Ashwagandha</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★★☆☆ | 20+ studies
<strong>Dose:</strong> 300-600mg daily
<strong>Cost:</strong> €15-25/month</p>
<p>Adaptogen that may reduce cortisol and improve recovery. Also shows modest strength gains in some studies.</p>
<p><a href="/blog/ashwagandha-strength-training-supplement-science-2026">Ashwagandha for lifters</a></p>
<h2 id="tier-3-possibly-works">Tier 3: Possibly Works</h2>
<h3 id="hmb">HMB</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★☆☆☆ | Mixed
<strong>Dose:</strong> 3g daily</p>
<p>May reduce muscle breakdown in untrained individuals. Less useful for experienced lifters.</p>
<h3 id="beta-ecdysterone">Beta-Ecdysterone</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★☆☆☆ | Limited
<strong>Dose:</strong> 500mg daily</p>
<p>Plant compound with some anabolic properties. Research is preliminary.</p>
<p><a href="/blog/beta-ecdysterone-muscle-anabolic-research">Evidence on ecdysterone</a></p>
<h3 id="taurine">Taurine</h3>
<p><strong>Evidence:</strong> ★★☆☆☆ | Limited
<strong>Dose:</strong> 2-3g</p>
<p>Popular but under-researched for strength. May aid hydration and recovery.</p>
<h2 id="tier-4-skip-these">Tier 4: Skip These</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>L-Carnitine:</strong> Great for fat loss narratives, weak for muscle</li>
<li><strong>BCAAs:</strong> Protein covers this</li>
<li><strong>Testosterone boosters:</strong> Most are underdosed or ineffective</li>
<li><strong>Generic "testosterone support" blends:</strong> Proprietary blends hide ineffective doses</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="bottom-line">Bottom Line</h2>
<p>Spend money on:</p>
<ol>
<li>Creatine (mandatory)</li>
<li>Caffeine (optional but effective)</li>
<li>Quality protein intake</li>
</ol>
<p>Everything else is optimization after you've nailed the basics.</p>
