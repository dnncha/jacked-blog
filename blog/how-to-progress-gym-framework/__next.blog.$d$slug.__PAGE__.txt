e:Td35,<p>Progressive overload — doing more over time — is the only non-negotiable for muscle growth. But "more" isn't always obvious.</p>
<h2 id="what-progressive-overload-actually-means">What Progressive Overload Actually Means</h2>
<p>More total volume over time. That can be:</p>
<ul>
<li>More weight on the bar</li>
<li>More reps at the same weight</li>
<li>More sets total</li>
<li>Less rest between sets</li>
</ul>
<p>The key: your muscles need a reason to grow. Same workout = same muscles.</p>
<h2 id="methods-from-simple-to-advanced">Methods: From Simple to Advanced</h2>
<h3 id="1-linear-progression-beginners">1. Linear Progression (Beginners)</h3>
<p>Add 2.5kg to the bar every session. Simple, effective for new lifters. Eventually stalls — that's normal.</p>
<h3 id="2-double-progression-intermediate">2. Double Progression (Intermediate)</h3>
<p>Hit the top of your rep range for all sets? Add weight. Hit the bottom? Stay there. Example: 3×8-12. When you hit 3×12, increase weight.</p>
<h3 id="3-rpe-based-progression-intermediate-advanced">3. RPE-Based Progression (Intermediate-Advanced)</h3>
<p>Rate effort on a 1-10 scale. Train at RPE 7-9. When 9 becomes easy, increase weight.</p>
<p><a href="/blog/autoregulation-vs-traditional-training">Learn about autoregulation</a></p>
<h3 id="4-block-periodization-advanced">4. Block Periodization (Advanced)</h3>
<p>Rotate training phases:</p>
<ul>
<li>Strength (low reps, high weight)</li>
<li>Hypertrophy (moderate reps)</li>
<li>Peaking (low volume, max intensity)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="5-auto-regulation-the-jacked-way">5. Auto-Regulation (The Jacked Way)</h3>
<p>Let your performance dictate your training. Good recovery = push harder. Feeling beat = deload. <a href="/">Jacked does this automatically.</a></p>
<h2 id="the-problem-with-traditional-programs">The Problem with Traditional Programs</h2>
<p>Fixed percentages ignore daily variation. You're not the same every day. Monday's 80% feels different than Friday's.</p>
<p><strong>Solution:</strong> Use RPE or rate of perceived exertion. Or let an app handle it.</p>
<h2 id="common-mistakes">Common Mistakes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Chasing numbers:</strong> 100kg on bench means nothing if you grind it</li>
<li><strong>Ignoring recovery:</strong> Progress requires rest</li>
<li><strong>Inconsistent tracking:</strong> Can't improve what you don't measure</li>
<li><strong>ego lifting:</strong> Technical breakdown kills gains and causes injury</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="practical-application">Practical Application</h2>
<p><strong>Week 1:</strong> 3×8 @ 70kg
<strong>Week 2:</strong> 3×8 @ 72.5kg<br>
<strong>Week 3:</strong> 3×8 @ 75kg
<strong>Week 4:</strong> 3×8 @ 77.5kg OR deload</p>
<p>When 8 reps becomes easy (RPE 6-7), increase weight by 2.5-5kg and drop back to 6 reps.</p>
<h2 id="the-bottom-line">The Bottom Line</h2>
<ol>
<li>Track everything</li>
<li>Add weight or reps when it gets easy</li>
<li>Don't ego lift</li>
<li>Sleep and eat enough</li>
<li>Be patient — gains come in months, not weeks</li>
</ol>
