e:Tc53,<p>Building muscle is simple but not easy. This guide covers the science-backed fundamentals that actually move the needle.</p>
<h2 id="training-the-mechanical-tension-foundation">Training: The Mechanical Tension Foundation</h2>
<p>Muscle growth requires mechanical tension. That means lifting heavy things close to failure. But "heavy" is relative — what matters is stimulating your muscles effectively.</p>
<p><strong>Key principles:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Progressive overload is non-negotiable</li>
<li>Training to within 2-3 reps of failure works best</li>
<li>Volume (total sets × reps × weight) drives growth</li>
<li>Eccentric training adds extra muscle damage and growth stimulus</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Practical application:</strong> Track your workouts. Without tracking, you can't progressive overload.</p>
<h2 id="nutrition-the-energy-and-building-blocks">Nutrition: The Energy and Building Blocks</h2>
<p>You need two things: enough protein and enough total calories.</p>
<p><strong>Protein:</strong> Aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg of bodyweight. More won't hurt, but won't help much either. Spread it across 3-4 meals.</p>
<p><strong>Calories:</strong> Surplus of 200-300 kcal is ideal. Too much and you'll gain excessive fat. Too few and muscle growth stalls.</p>
<p><strong>Timing:</strong> The "anabolic window" is a myth. Total daily intake matters more than when you eat.</p>
<h2 id="recovery-where-growth-actually-happens">Recovery: Where Growth Actually Happens</h2>
<p>Training breaks muscle down. Recovery builds it back up.</p>
<p><strong>Sleep:</strong> 7-9 hours. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Skimp here and you leave gains on the table.</p>
<p><strong>Protein synthesis:</strong> Maximized every 3-5 hours after protein intake. Three solid meals plus a protein-rich snack covers this.</p>
<p><strong>Deloads:</strong> Every 4-8 weeks, reduce volume by 40-50% for a week. Fresh muscles grow faster.</p>
<h2 id="supplementation-what-actually-works">Supplementation: What Actually Works</h2>
<p><strong>Creatine monohydrate:</strong> The most researched supplement. 5g daily. Works.</p>
<p><strong>Caffeine:</strong> Performance enhancer. 100-200mg pre-workout. Don't rely on it constantly.</p>
<p><strong>Beta-alanine:</strong> Buffer lactic acid. 3-5g daily. Tingling sensation is normal.</p>
<p><strong>Whey protein:</strong> Convenient way to hit protein targets. Not magic, just convenient.</p>
<h2 id="the-bottom-line">The Bottom Line</h2>
<ol>
<li>Train with intensity and track everything</li>
<li>Eat 1.8g protein per kg bodyweight</li>
<li>Sleep 8 hours</li>
<li>Add creatine</li>
<li>Be patient — muscle takes months, not weeks</li>
</ol>
<p>Start here. Master these fundamentals. Then optimize.</p>
